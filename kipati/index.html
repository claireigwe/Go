Why Design Kipati?

Financial institutions worldwide lose a whooping $3.6 billion to penalties for non-compliance

Kipati was an effort to unravel the source of this failure, and leverage technology to prevent non-compliance.

Non-compliance can hurt a business' customers, reputation and profits. The biggest source of this failure is a breach in communication and a lack of accountability.

Decisions Reached from UX Research

Based on the insights gathered from interviewing compliance experts and business managers, I identified five (5) crucial factors that increase the risk of non-compliance.

Outdated GRC Tools

Managing the evolving compliance requirements and business landscape with the same old tool is ineffective

Leadership Lapses

Business often lack leaders that prioritize compliance and inspire a cultural shift accross the organization

Evolving Requirements

Compliance standards are constantkly shifting and complicating practices

Gaps in Communication

Players are unsure or unaware of their roles and requirements

Multiple Players

Compliance is complicated by the existence of multiple players

Leveraging insights from UX Research, we weighed our options with the software engineer. The team identified potential product solutions, the technologies required for the development of these alternatives, and the team's technical and procedural limitations.

Afterwards, I mapped out the diverse product options on an Impact-Complexity Matrix to define a Minimum Viable Product as show above. The design solution would focus on
Workflow Automation, Dependency Management and an effective Communication
