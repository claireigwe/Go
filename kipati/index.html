Stay Compliant with Timely Reminders from one SaaS

Kipati is a B2B compliance management SaaS built for Nigerian businesses constantly joggling multiple compliance requirements and deadlines

Product Overview

Design Challenge

Before Kipati, businesses in Nigeria joggled multiple compliance deadlines and sent out reminders to the diverse stakeholders manually. This lack of automation often resulted in missed deadlines and all the resulting penalties.

Design Solution and Process

I combined insights from competitor analyses, the product founder, and compliance management experts to design a high-fidelity prototype for a cloud-based business reminder automation software. The goal was to optimize flexibility and efficiency of use, build intuitive and minimal interfaces, and deliver easy-to-code UI/UX designs.

The Challenge

Why Kipati

So, why do businesses keep missing compliance deadlines?

Beyond the financial penalties, businesses also risk criminal prosecution and the irredeemable loss of reputation from missed compliance deadlines

Managing compliance becomes inefficient when it involves:

  • Multiple Reminders
  • Multiple Requirements
  • Multiple Stakeholders

The Solution

Why Kipati

MVP

Forms were designed to be consistent with the patterns on popular email apps. With Kipati, managers could handle reminders for multiple recipients as easily as regular emails.

There were two connected aspects to the design:

  • Website Design - For Marketing
  • Desktop Application Design

Design Thinking

The sytematic process behind the thoughts that went into Kipati

Empathize

Stakeholder interviews and competitor analyses exposed how the ineffective communication of roles, requirements, and deadlines led to compliance failures

Ideate

After brainstorming with the product founder, the developed the initial concepts for a compliance reminder software that kept all the stakeholders in the loop

Prototype

We created high-fidelity wireframes for a desktop sofware's landing page, Sign Up/Sign In screens, User Dashboard, and Reminder Setting screens

Test

With a high-fidelity prototype, we evaluated our product's effectiveness and ease of use with respect to design heuristic principles

The Process: UX Research

The Product Designer's Process from Drawing Insights

UX Research Method

Due to the timeline project, the trade-off was to draw insights from stakeholder interviews and competitor analyses only (no semi-structured interviews and surveys).

Pain Points

My UX research revealed that businesses failed to meet compliance deadlines because of a breach in communication between the diverse actors that were to perform different activities at various stages of compliance fulfillment
